Have UK Covid-19 Deaths really overtaken the EU as a whole?

The Daily Mirror says so, Facebook too, but lets have a closer look...

The charts show the Deaths from Covid-19 across the EU.

I have created a sum total for all of the 27 EU states excluding the UK, and graphed this against the UK daily total. I also wanted to create two break out charts showing the spanish adjustment to their count, and the rather shocking confirmation of the Daily Mirror article.

The Overall Picture

Obviously, the EU is a rather large geographical area, with a huge population (446 million inhabitants - the worldâ€™s third largest population after China and India) so it's unsurprising that the peak UK deaths were lower at the beginning of the pandemic (The UK population was estimated to be 66,796,807 in mid-2019.) Although the drop off of the EU deaths is significantly steeper, which may be due to stricter lockdown measures.

Data from europa.eu - the EU's open data portal

The EU makes getting hold of this data very easy, which was impressive. They provide CSV, JSON and XML, being up for a challenge I took the CSV over the JSON so I'd need to muck about with it a bit more.

Spanish Death Reporting Change

On the 25/05/2020 Spain adjusted it's death reporting (hence the very negative EU figures). I'm not sure what this really means for our data, whether the Spanish were previously over-reporting deaths, or from this point on they are under-reporting...

UK Death Rate overtakes the EU as a whole!

The shocking result that the Daily Mirror was correct, and that on the 2nd, 4th and 6th June, the UK daily deaths were indeed higher than the EU as a whole.
